Project Overview

Based on findings from WyldFyrez (Project 1), California and Georgia were found to have the highest number of fires by state. To better understand, we map the causes of the fires and their locations in each state for visual learning. After this, we use supplemental data, such as average, maximum and minimum monthly temperatures, and attempt to predict two features of fires: cause and size category.

Making Predictions with Machine Learning

We are attempting to find relationships and their predictability between features of our dataset. Two of the features being predicted include the cause of the fire and the size category of the fires. The cause of the fire is not likely going to get a very good prediction rate due to fires not strongly linked to many of the features of wildfires. However, the fire size category will likely have a higher prediction accuracy rate as weather will likely strongly correlate with the size of a fire.

Data Used for California and Georgia

Fire size, year and month, average monthly temperature, maximum monthly temperature, minimum monthly temperature, and fire cause.
